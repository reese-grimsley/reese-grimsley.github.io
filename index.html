Systems Research and

Track the Intractable

I am a first year student at Carngie Mellon University, currently splitting my time between the Silicon Valley satellite campus and the Pittsburgh main campus. I am co-advised by Bob Iannucci and Anthony Rowe in the groups CCSG and WISELab, respectively.

I am currently focused on building vertical system prototypes for wide-scale sensing platforms within the Internet of Things. A hallmark of my experiences to-date is my endless pursuit in enumerating the parameters we have to consider as we build such systems. Let's just say it's a good thing digital notebooks don't have a page limit!

My goal is to build a career in large-scale sensor networks that overhaul the way we monitor natural environments and urban infrastructure. In Dr. Iannucci's group, we have an ongoing partnership with the U.S. Geological Survey and Pepperwood Preserve, which involves LP-WAN enabled stream gauge sensors for reporting anamolies in headwaters of the Russian River Basin. I am hoping to push future systems research towards traffic sensing and infrastructure monitoring, as I feel this area has great opportunity for academic research and societal impact. I would love to build V2X infrastructure that narrows the gap between ever-improving autonomous vehicles, which sense their surroundings from an insulated chassis, and the traffic lights that dictate vehicle flows.

In a word, my IoT systems research focuses on flexibility. Within current projects, this manifests itself as remote reprogrammability, in which embedded devices are reprogrammed at the application level, leaving the bulk of (largely immuatable) firmware intact. Without oversharing, we're delving into reprogramming of resource constrained devices in federated cyber physical systems, meaning that the system is owned by no single entity. It is "federated" in that an arbitrating body oversees information exchange and resource sharing without dictating the purpose of the system; the purpose is to build IoT for the benefit of communities. Continuations on this idea of "IoT Flexibility" may dive deeper into this seemingly bottomless hole, or it may lend itself towards something spectrum collaboration, in which networks actively collaborate to effectively share resources such as RF bands and time-on-the-air. We shall see where the pursuit of knowledge takes me!
